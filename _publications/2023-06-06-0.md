Digital twins for the water sector are opening new methods of research for water engineers. Languages like Python with its rich set of plug-in package functionality make it relatively straightforward for researchers to take raw data and process it using a rich variety of off -the-shelf data classification, evaluation, and clustering models to create information and knowledge from data, before presenting it using a variety of presentation frameworks. This approach creates two clear challenges: 1) proving that complex data processing pipelines work as intended and 2) collecting the ‘right’ kind of source data, particularly for edge cases and leaks. In this work, we are reviewing our experiences from aqua3S, Fiware4Water and LOTUS to show how we have developed and used synthetic data approaches to 1) demonstrate data integrity across FIWARE-based data processing pipelines, 2) demonstrate how volume data can be processed within real world time constraints and 3) create suitable edge-case data for testing the operation of algorithms. Through our work on these projects, we have started to develop a wealth of best-practice, strategicchoices, and approaches to be avoided for successful and long-term context broker-based data management.
