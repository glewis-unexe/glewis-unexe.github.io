FIWARE is a great piece of technology and forms the data hub of recent digital twin-based research projects that University of Exeter has been involved with (aqua3S, Fiware4Water, LOTUS and Waterverse). FIWARE’s http-based interface gives users a  mall vocabulary of functions to work data through the data CRUD lifecycle to create, retrieve, update, and delete data in comparison to SQL or no-SQL approaches. However, FIWARE does have some weaknesses that tend to get exposed during development activities rather than operations, which can create a lot of issues for developers. FIWARE’s http-based approach can make it difficult to onboard large amounts of historic data into a broker in a timely manner, something which can be essential when looking to  test algorithms that require large amounts of historic broker data. The purpose of this paper is to document an approach taken to implementing FIWARE ngsi-ld/v1 functionality to make it more suitable for the development and testing of digital twins, rather than their operation.
