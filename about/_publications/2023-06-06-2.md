The water industry is often and unfairly criticised for being relatively slow to adopt IT as part of its operating processes. To address this, the aqua3S project was tasked with developing open-source data definition, management, and processing, and applying them to the water sector. The aqua3S project sought to develop a data-centric approach using a FIWARE broker as a data hub for seven water distribution operator pilots across Europe and develop approaches for standardisation, security, and safety in the water distribution sector. The purpose of this paper is to record the technical decisions, development activities and results generated for the work that was undertaken by University of Exeter in developing the visualisation, early warning, and decision support components of the project, demonstrate how these components integrate with the aqua3S theoretical goals of standardisation, security, and safety, and, moreover, demonstrate how the components integrate with the aqua3S FIWARE-centric broker architecture.
